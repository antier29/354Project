Javascript supports scientific notation


2.998e8 will be written as 299800000
2.998e-3 will be written as 0.002998

Infinity ∞ and Negative Infinity - ∞ are used to know if you have gone higher or lower than the largest possible number in Javascript. That number is 1.797693131348623157e+308. Now that's a big number!

Plus, Negative and Logical Not

The plus sign ( + ), converts strings of numbers, booleans and null's to numbers. The negative sign ( - ) does the exact same thing but negates it. 

    + 50 and + '50' will give the result of 50.
    + '-50' will give the result of -50.
    if you use the negative sign...
    - 50 will give you -50
    - '-50' will give you 50.

True will give you a result of 1 and null will give you a result of 0. So if you were to negate them, you would get -1 and -0. Yes I said -0! It is a weird thing to see but just think of it as any other number.

	delete myObj.minor

Hoisting

Hoisting is when a browser/engine compiles variables and named-functions immediately.You are bringing them to the top of the file. When Javascript compiles all of your code, all var declarations are hoisted/lifted to the top of their functional/local scope of an inside function or to the top of their global scope if its an outside function regardless of where the actual declaration has been made.
